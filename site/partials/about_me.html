Take yourself back to the year 1893. Imagine yourself in a secluded valley that is surrounded by mountains and greenery. Here, you see bison, along with other wildlife you may have never seen before. The cattle, crisp air, and stunning views have you sucked in. Now, where does the name "Jackson Hole" come from? The town was named after a beaver trapper, David Edward Jackson. Jackson increased the region's exposure to northerners who had never been brave enough to make a pit-stop to this region. Its winters were known for being severely cold, and Jackson was able to make it through an entire winter.
